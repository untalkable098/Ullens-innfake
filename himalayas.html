The Himalayas are a vast and majestic mountain range in Asia, stretching across Nepal, India, Bhutan, China, and Pakistan. They are the highest mountains in the world and include Mount Everest, the tallest peak on Earth. Formed millions of years ago by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, the Himalayas hold deep cultural, spiritual, and ecological significance, providing diverse habitats and being revered in many local traditions.

The Himalayas

Annapurna

  • Destination: Annapurna Base Camp (4,130 m / 13,550 ft)
  • Duration: Typically 7-12 days (depending on route and pace)
  • Starting point: Pokhara → Nayapul / Jhinu / Ghandruk (varies by itinerary)
  • Difficulty: Moderate (challenging mainly due to altitude and long daily walks)
  • Best seasons:
    • Spring (Mar-May): Rhododendrons, stable weather Autumn (Sep-Nov): Clear skies, best mountain views

Mt.Everest

  • Destination: Everest Base Camp (5,364 m / 17,598 ft)
  • Duration: Typically 12–14 days (depending on acclimatization and pace)
  • Starting point: Kathmandu → Lukla (flight) → Phakding
  • Difficulty: Moderate to challenging
    (primarily due to extreme altitude, cold conditions, and long trekking days)
  • Best seasons:
    • Spring (Mar-May): Warmer temperatures, expedition season, busy trails
    Autumn (Sep-Nov): Stable weather, clear skies, best visibility of
    Everest and surrounding peaks
